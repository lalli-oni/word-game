# Game Philosophy: Word Connection

The core philosophy of **Word Connection** is to create a "Multidimensional Word Puzzle." Most word games are "One-Way": they either test your spelling, your vocabulary, or your knowledge of meanings. This game forces the player to navigate a hybrid space where all these systems are interconnected.

## Our Goal
We want to challenge players to switch between different cognitive modes:
1. **Structural Mode**: Recognizing that "CAT" is one letter away from "BAT".
2. **Anagram Mode**: Seeing that "ARC" can become "CAR".
3. **Semantic Mode**: Realizing that "HAPPY" can "teleport" you to "SAD" or "GLAD", which may then open up entirely new structural opportunities.

The "Shortest Path" challenge turns a word list into a map. Players aren't just looking for words; they're looking for *bridges*.

## Comparison with Similar Games

| Game | Mechanic | Comparison to Word Connection |
|------|----------|-------------------------------|
| **Word Ladders / Weaver** | Spelling (Change 1 letter) | This is our "Structural Mode," but it's only one of our dimensions. Word ladders can be repetitive; we add "teleportation" via synonyms. |
| **Semantle / Contexto** | Meaning (Semantic distance) | These focus on "getting closer" in meaning. We use meaning as a *move*, which might be a shortcut to a structural path. |
| **Anagram Games** | Structure (Rearranging letters) | These are often isolated puzzles. In our game, an anagram is a tactical maneuver to change your current word's structure for a better follow-up. |
| **The Wiki Game** | Navigation (Hyperlinks) | The Wiki Game is the closest in *spirit*. We treat the English language like Wikipedia, where words are "pages" linked by their letters, their origins, and their meanings. |

## Why it Matters
By combining these mechanics, we create a game where there is no "fixed" solution path. Every player might take a completely different route from "COLD" to "WARM" depending on their own linguistic intuition and structural pattern recognition. 

This hybrid approach makes **Word Connection** more than just a word game; it's a **linguistic navigation puzzle**.
